# 5. Fourth Generation: Adoptive Cell Therapies and Next-Generation Platforms (2017--Present)

## 5.1 Historical Context

Building on the immunotherapy revolution, the fourth generation represents the most personalized and engineered approaches to cancer treatment. CAR-T cell therapy was approved in 2017, and the development of bispecific antibodies, antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs), and novel vaccine platforms continues to expand the therapeutic arsenal.

## 5.2 Major Drug Classes

### 5.2.1 CAR-T Cell Therapy

- **Mechanism:** Patient's T cells are extracted, genetically engineered to express a chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) targeting a tumor-specific antigen, expanded ex vivo, and reinfused.
- **Approved products:**
  - *Tisagenlecleucel (Kymriah):* FDA approved 2017 for pediatric/young adult B-ALL.
  - *Axicabtagene ciloleucel (Yescarta):* Approved 2017 for large B-cell lymphoma.
  - *Lisocabtagene maraleucel (Breyanzi), idecabtagene vicleucel (Abecma), ciltacabtagene autoleucel (Carvykti):* Subsequent approvals for lymphoma and multiple myeloma.
- **Clinical impact:** Complete remission rates of 50--90% in relapsed/refractory hematological malignancies.

### 5.2.2 Bispecific Antibodies

- **Mechanism:** Simultaneously bind a tumor antigen and a T-cell activator (typically CD3), physically bridging immune effector cells to cancer cells.
- **Four classes:** Immune effector cell redirectors, tumor-targeted immunomodulators, dual immunomodulators, and dual tumor-targeting bispecific antibodies.
- **Examples:**
  - *Blinatumomab (Blincyto):* CD19xCD3 bispecific T-cell engager for B-ALL (approved 2014).
  - *Cadonilimab:* PD-1 x CTLA-4 bispecific, first approved bispecific antibody targeting dual inhibitory checkpoints.
  - *Mosunetuzumab, epcoritamab, glofitamab:* CD20xCD3 bispecifics for B-cell lymphomas.

### 5.2.3 Antibody-Drug Conjugates (ADCs)

**Mechanism:** A monoclonal antibody (the "guided missile") is chemically linked to a cytotoxic small-molecule payload (the "warhead") via a linker. After binding to its target antigen on the tumor cell surface, the ADC is internalized, the linker is cleaved, and the payload is released intracellularly — killing the cancer cell while sparing normal tissue.

Three structural variables define ADC performance:
- **Antibody:** determines target specificity and tumor selectivity
- **Linker:** cleavable (pH-sensitive, enzymatic) or non-cleavable; determines stability in circulation and payload release kinetics
- **Payload:** cytotoxic potency and mechanism (tubulin inhibitor, topoisomerase I inhibitor, DNA alkylator)
- **Drug-to-antibody ratio (DAR):** average number of payload molecules per antibody; too low = insufficient potency; too high = aggregation and toxicity

#### Generational Evolution of ADCs

| Generation | Era | Linker Type | Payload | DAR | Key Example | Limitation |
|-----------|-----|------------|---------|-----|------------|------------|
| 1st Gen | 2000–2012 | Hydrazone (acid-cleavable) | Calicheamicin, maytansinoid (DM1) | ~3 | Gemtuzumab ozogamicin (CD33, AML) | Unstable linker; off-target release; narrow therapeutic index |
| 2nd Gen | 2011–2019 | Maleimide-thiol (reducible) | MMAE, MMAF (tubulin inhibitors), DM1 | 3–4 | T-DM1 (HER2, breast); Brentuximab vedotin (CD30, lymphoma) | Heterogeneous DAR; some bystander toxicity |
| 3rd Gen | 2019–present | Tetrapeptide cleavable (enzyme) | DXd (topoisomerase I inhibitor, exatecan derivative) | 7–8 | Trastuzumab deruxtecan / Enhertu (HER2) | Higher DAR enables bystander effect; ILD risk |
| 4th Gen (emerging) | 2023–present | Site-specific conjugation; dual-target linkers | Immune-stimulating payloads (ISACs), bispecific ADCs, α-emitter payloads | Variable | Bispecific ADCs (AZ/DS), ISAC platforms | Manufacturing complexity; efficacy/safety still being defined |

**The Enhertu paradigm shift (3rd Gen):** Trastuzumab deruxtecan demonstrated that high-DAR ADCs with a potent topoisomerase I inhibitor payload and a cleavable peptide linker can kill not just the targeted cell but also neighboring tumor cells with lower target expression — the **"bystander effect."** This enabled efficacy in **HER2-low breast cancer** (IHC 1+ or 2+ without amplification), a previously untreated population representing ~50% of all breast cancers. The DESTINY-Breast04 trial (2022) established a new disease category and set the benchmark for next-generation ADC design.

#### Competitive Landscape and M&A

The ADC market has become the most acquisition-active segment in pharma:

- **Pfizer / Seagen** ($43B acquisition, 2023): Seagen's vedotin platform (MMAE payload, maleimide-thiol linker) now integrated into Pfizer oncology
- **Gilead / Immunomedics** ($21B, 2020): Sacituzumab govitecan and TROP-2 platform
- **AbbVie / ImmunoGen** ($10.1B, 2023): Mirvetuximab soravtansine (FRα) and IMGN platform
- **Daiichi Sankyo / AstraZeneca** (up to $6.9B deal, 2023): Three DXd-based ADCs (trastuzumab deruxtecan, datopotamab deruxtecan, patritumab deruxtecan) in a co-development/commercialization agreement

#### Fourth-Generation ADC Concepts (Emerging)

- **Bispecific ADCs:** Antibody portion targets two antigens simultaneously, improving tumor selectivity and reducing target-loss resistance. ZW49 (HER2 biparatopic), MEDI4276 (two HER2 epitopes).
- **Immune-stimulating ADCs (ISACs):** Payload is a TLR agonist or STING agonist rather than a cytotoxin — kills tumor AND activates local immune response. Early clinical stage.
- **Site-specific conjugation:** Enzymatic (transglutaminase, sortase) or unnatural amino acid incorporation achieves homogeneous DAR, improving PK and reducing batch variability.
- **α-emitter ADCs:** Replacing cytotoxic payload with actinium-225 or bismuth-213 for targeted radioimmunotherapy.

#### Resistance Mechanisms

1. **Antigen downregulation or loss:** Tumor cells reduce surface expression of the ADC target
2. **Impaired internalization:** Reduced receptor recycling or endosomal trafficking
3. **Lysosomal dysfunction:** Impaired payload release due to altered acidification
4. **MDR1/P-gp efflux:** Drug pumps expel released payload (especially MMAE)
5. **DNA repair upregulation:** In topoisomerase I inhibitor ADCs

### 5.2.4 mRNA Cancer Vaccines and Neoantigen Therapies

- **Mechanism:** Personalized vaccines encoding tumor-specific neoantigens, stimulating de novo anti-tumor immune responses.
- **Status:** Multiple candidates in late-stage clinical trials (e.g., mRNA-4157/V940 in combination with pembrolizumab for melanoma).

## 5.3 Limitations

- **CAR-T:** Cytokine release syndrome (CRS), neurotoxicity, manufacturing complexity, high cost ($300K--$500K per treatment), limited efficacy in solid tumors.
- **Bispecifics:** CRS risk, limited durability in some settings.
- **ADCs:** Toxicity from linker instability (off-target payload release), resistance through antigen downregulation.
