Human heredity

When we say "human DNA," which DNA do we mean?

Usually we mean nuclear DNA: chromosomes inside the nucleus. But a human cell also carries mitochondrial DNA inside mitochondria. The two DNA systems are inherited through different physical routes: chromosomes from both parents, mitochondria mostly from the egg.

The Meaning Of "My DNA"

In everyday speech, "my DNA" nearly always points to the nuclear genome. In complete cell biology, inherited human genetic material includes both nuclear DNA and mtDNA.

Two Genetic Systems, Two Visual Shapes

The nuclear genome is organized as linear chromosomes. The mitochondrial genome is a small circular genome inside mitochondria.

How The Two DNA Systems Are Delivered

Fertilization transfers a sperm nuclear genome into an egg cell. The egg contributes its own nuclear genome plus the cytoplasm and mitochondria that seed the embryo.

Two Hereditary Channels

The key correction is that heredity transfers a cell, not just abstract sequence text.

Nuclear heredity

  • Delivered by egg nucleus and sperm nucleus.
  • One chromosome set from each parent.
  • Explains most Mendelian inheritance.
  • Recombination mixes grandparental chromosome segments.

Mitochondrial heredity

  • Delivered mainly by egg cytoplasm.
  • Mitochondria carry mtDNA copies.
  • Follows the maternal line in standard human inheritance.
  • Many mtDNA copies can segregate unevenly.

Integrated child cell

  • The zygote contains both systems.
  • Nuclear genes encode most mitochondrial proteins.
  • Mitochondrial genes encode a small local energy-system set.
  • Both systems must work together in one cell.

Why mtDNA Inheritance Can Be Uneven

A cell can contain many mtDNA molecules. If all copies are the same, that is homoplasmy. If there is a mixture, that is heteroplasmy.

Summary

The sentence "we inherit DNA from both parents" is true for nuclear DNA. It is incomplete for the whole cell.
