Cell Structure And Mitochondria

A cell is not a bag of parts. It is a compartmented, energy-managed, information-reading system in which membranes, the nucleus, ribosomes, mitochondria, lysosomes, cytoskeleton, and plant- or animal-specific structures constrain one another. This report explains the major structures of eukaryotic cells, why mitochondria are central to the rest of the cell rather than just "powerhouses," and how animal and plant cells share a core architecture while solving different life problems.
