Core Cell Structure

  • OpenStax. Biology 2e, 4.3 Eukaryotic Cells. Used for the overview of plasma membrane, nucleus, ribosomes, mitochondria, lysosomes, cytoskeleton, and plant/animal cell differences.
  • OpenStax. Biology 2e, 4.4 The Endomembrane System and Proteins. Used for rough ER, smooth ER, Golgi, and protein routing.

NCBI Bookshelf And Molecular Cell Biology

  • Cooper GM. The Cell: A Molecular Approach. 2nd edition. The Origin and Evolution of Cells. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for eukaryotic cell organization, nucleus/cytoplasm separation, mitochondria, chloroplasts, lysosomes, vacuoles, ER, Golgi, and cytoskeleton.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. The Compartmentalization of Cells. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for compartment logic, membrane-enclosed organelles, protein targeting, and organelle inheritance.
  • Cooper GM. The Cell: A Molecular Approach. 2nd edition. Cell Membranes. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for selective permeability and transport across phospholipid bilayers.
  • Cooper GM. The Cell: A Molecular Approach. 2nd edition. Structure of the Plasma Membrane. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for membrane proteins, lipid/protein composition, and fluid mosaic model framing.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. Membrane Structure. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for lipid bilayers, membrane proteins, and membrane functions.
  • Cooper GM. The Cell: A Molecular Approach. 2nd edition. Translation of mRNA. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for ribosome and translation mechanics.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. From RNA to Protein. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for ribosome as catalytic translation machine.
  • Cooper GM. The Cell: A Molecular Approach. 2nd edition. Mitochondria. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for mitochondrial structure, endosymbiotic origin, mitochondrial genome, and nuclear-encoded mitochondrial proteins.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. The Genetic Systems of Mitochondria and Plastids. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for mitochondria/plastid genetic systems, gene transfer, and nuclear control of organelle biogenesis.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. Energy Conversion: Mitochondria and Chloroplasts. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for chemiosmosis, ATP synthesis, and endosymbiotic dependence on nuclear-encoded proteins.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. The Transport of Proteins into Mitochondria and Chloroplasts. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for post-translational import of nuclear-encoded mitochondrial proteins.
  • Cooper GM. The Cell: A Molecular Approach. 2nd edition. Lysosomes. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for acidic lysosomal degradation and recycling.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. Transport from the Trans Golgi Network to Lysosomes. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for endosome/lysosome maturation, lysosomal traffic, and autophagy.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. The Self-Assembly and Dynamic Structure of Cytoskeletal Filaments. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for dynamic cytoskeletal filaments.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. Molecular Motors. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for kinesin, dynein, myosin, and organelle transport.
  • Alberts B, Johnson A, Lewis J, et al. Molecular Biology of the Cell. 4th edition. Chromosomal DNA and Its Packaging in the Chromatin Fiber. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for nucleosomes, histone octamers, chromatin fiber organization, and chromosome packing.
  • Cooper GM. The Cell: A Molecular Approach. 2nd edition. Chromosomes and Chromatin. NCBI Bookshelf. Used for nucleosome structure, histone H1, chromatin fiber, and chromosome condensation.

Classic And Historical Sources

  • Singer SJ, Nicolson GL. The fluid mosaic model of the structure of cell membranes. Science. 1972;175(4023):720-731. Used for the classic membrane model reference.
  • Sagan L. On the origin of mitosing cells. Journal of Theoretical Biology. 1967;14(3):255-274. Used as the classic serial endosymbiosis reference associated with Lynn Margulis.
  • Martin W, Mentel M. The origin of mitochondria. Nature. 2010;466:920-922. Used as a modern review pointer on mitochondrial origin.
  • NobelPrize.org. The 2016 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine - Advanced Information. Used for autophagy background and the autophagosome-to-lysosome recycling framing.
  • Pickles S, Vigie P, Youle RJ. Mitophagy and Quality Control Mechanisms in Mitochondrial Maintenance. Current Biology. 2018;28(4):R170-R185. Used for mitophagy and mitochondrial quality-control framing.
  • Banani SF, Lee HO, Hyman AA, Rosen MK. Biomolecular condensates: organizers of cellular biochemistry. Nature Reviews Molecular Cell Biology. 2017;18:285-298. Used for membraneless organelles and phase-separation framing.
  • Ginhoux F, Guilliams M. Tissue-Resident Macrophage Ontogeny and Homeostasis. Immunity. 2016;44(3):439-449. Used for monocyte-derived versus embryonically seeded tissue-resident macrophage origin.
