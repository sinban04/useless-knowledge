# ATP, Energy, and Kinases

A structured deep-dive into a single molecule — adenosine triphosphate (ATP) — and why it sits at the intersection of two seemingly unrelated jobs in every living cell: **delivering energy** to power reactions, and **delivering information** by handing a phosphate to a protein. The bridge between those two jobs is a family of \~518 enzymes called **kinases**, and almost every signal your body uses to decide what a cell should do passes through one of them.

This report is built so a reader who has forgotten everything about biochemistry can pick up any chapter cold and follow it from first principles.
