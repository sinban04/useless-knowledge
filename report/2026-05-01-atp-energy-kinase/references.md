# References

This report draws from textbooks, primary research literature, structural databases, and curated kinase resources. The references below are grouped by chapter relevance.

---

## Textbooks (Foundation)

- **Lehninger Principles of Biochemistry**, 8th ed. (Nelson, Cox). Comprehensive coverage of ATP structure, hydrolysis thermodynamics, glycolysis, TCA, OXPHOS, ATP synthase. The standard reference for chapters 2-4.
- **Molecular Biology of the Cell**, 7th ed. (Alberts et al.). Best textbook coverage of signal transduction, kinase cascades, GPCRs, RTKs. Strong for chapters 5-7.
- **Biochemistry**, 9th ed. (Berg, Tymoczko, Gatto, Stryer). Especially good on enzyme mechanism and kinase chemistry. Strong for chapters 5-6.
- **The Cell: A Molecular Approach**, 8th ed. (Cooper). Concise alternative to Alberts.

## Primary Literature — ATP, Phosphate, and ATP Synthase (Chapters 2-4)

- **Atkinson DE (1968).** "Energy charge of the adenylate pool as a regulatory parameter. Interaction with feedback modifiers." *Biochemistry* 7:4030-4034. Original adenylate energy charge framework.
- **Westheimer FH (1987).** "Why Nature Chose Phosphates." *Science* 235:1173-1178. Classic explanation of phosphate chemistry in biology.
- **Wallimann T, Wyss M, Brdiczka D, Nicolay K, Eppenberger HM (1992).** "Intracellular compartmentation, structure and function of creatine kinase isoenzymes in tissues with high and fluctuating energy demands: the phosphocreatine circuit for cellular energy homeostasis." *Biochem J* 281:21-40. Review of the phosphocreatine circuit.
- **Mitchell P (1961).** "Coupling of phosphorylation to electron and hydrogen transfer by a chemi-osmotic type of mechanism." *Nature* 191:144-148. The chemiosmotic hypothesis.
- **Frey TG, Mannella CA (2000).** "The internal structure of mitochondria." *Trends Biochem Sci* 25:319-324. EM tomography view of cristae and mitochondrial compartment organization.
- **Cogliati S, Enriquez JA, Scorrano L (2016).** "Mitochondrial Cristae: Where Beauty Meets Functionality." *Trends Biochem Sci* 41:261-273. Review of cristae shape, OXPHOS organization, and bioenergetic function.
- **Boyer PD (1993).** "The binding change mechanism for ATP synthase — some probabilities and possibilities." *Biochim Biophys Acta* 1140:215-250. Boyer's binding-change mechanism.
- **Abrahams JP, Leslie AGW, Lutter R, Walker JE (1994).** "Structure at 2.8 Å resolution of F1-ATPase from bovine heart mitochondria." *Nature* 370:621-628. The first F1 crystal structure.
- **Noji H, Yasuda R, Yoshida M, Kinosita K Jr (1997).** "Direct observation of the rotation of F1-ATPase." *Nature* 386:299-302. Single-molecule visualization of F1 rotation.
- **Kuhlbrandt W (2019).** "Structure and Mechanisms of F-Type ATP Synthases." *Annu Rev Biochem* 88:515-549. Modern structural biology review.

## Primary Literature — Kinase Structure and Mechanism (Chapter 5)

- **Knighton DR et al. (1991).** "Crystal structure of the catalytic subunit of cyclic adenosine monophosphate-dependent protein kinase." *Science* 253:407-414. The first protein-kinase crystal structure (PKA).
- **Hanks SK, Hunter T (1995).** "The eukaryotic protein kinase superfamily: kinase (catalytic) domain structure and classification." *FASEB J* 9:576-596. The conserved-motif framework.
- **Taylor SS, Kornev AP (2011).** "Protein kinases: evolution of dynamic regulatory proteins." *Trends Biochem Sci* 36:65-77. The "regulatory spine" and "catalytic spine" concepts.
- **Kornev AP, Taylor SS (2010).** "Defining the conserved internal architecture of a protein kinase." *Biochim Biophys Acta* 1804:440-444. Spine analysis.
- **Arter C, Trask L, Ward S, Yeoh S, Bayliss R (2022).** "Structural features of the protein kinase domain and targeted binding by small-molecule inhibitors." *J Biol Chem* 298:102247. Clear structural overview of kinase lobes, ATP site, C-helix, activation loop, and inhibitor pockets.
- **Turjanski AG, Hummer G, Gutkind JS (2009).** "How Mitogen-Activated Protein Kinases Recognize and Phosphorylate Their Targets: A QM/MM Study." *J Am Chem Soc* 131:6141-6148. Atom-level model of kinase phosphoryl transfer from ATP to a target peptide.
- **Roskoski R Jr (2024).** "Properties of FDA-approved small molecule protein kinase inhibitors: a 2024 update." *Pharmacol Res* 200:107059. Comprehensive recent review.

## Primary Literature — The Kinome (Chapter 5-6)

- **Manning G, Whyte DB, Martinez R, Hunter T, Sudarsanam S (2002).** "The protein kinase complement of the human genome." *Science* 298:1912-1934. The 518-kinase catalog and phylogenetic tree.
- **Hunter T (2000).** "Signaling — 2000 and beyond." *Cell* 100:113-127. Foundational essay on kinase signaling.

## Primary Literature — Phosphorylation as Signaling (Chapter 6)

- **Cohen P (2002).** "The origins of protein phosphorylation." *Nat Cell Biol* 4:E127-E130. Historical perspective.
- **Hopfield JJ (1974).** "Kinetic proofreading: a new mechanism for reducing errors in biosynthetic processes requiring high specificity." *Proc Natl Acad Sci USA* 71:4135-4139. The canonical analysis of energy-fidelity coupling.
- **Olsen JV et al. (2006).** "Global, in vivo, and site-specific phosphorylation dynamics in signaling networks." *Cell* 127:635-648. Quantitative phospho-proteomics.
- **Hornbeck PV et al. (2015).** "PhosphoSitePlus, 2014: mutations, PTMs and recalibrations." *Nucleic Acids Res* 43:D512-D520. The PhosphoSitePlus database.

## Primary Literature — Signaling Cascades (Chapter 7)

### PKA / cAMP

- **Sutherland EW, Rall TW (1958).** "Fractionation and characterization of a cyclic adenine ribonucleotide formed by tissue particles." *J Biol Chem* 232:1077-1091. Discovery of cAMP.
- **Krebs EG, Fischer EH (1956).** "The phosphorylase b to a converting enzyme of rabbit skeletal muscle." *Biochim Biophys Acta* 20:150-157. Original phosphorylase kinase paper.
- **Taylor SS et al. (2012).** "Assembly of allosteric macromolecular switches: lessons from PKA." *Nat Rev Mol Cell Biol* 13:646-658.

### MAPK

- **Marshall CJ (1995).** "Specificity of receptor tyrosine kinase signaling: transient versus sustained extracellular signal-regulated kinase activation." *Cell* 80:179-185. Duration-encoded signaling.
- **Lavoie H, Gagnon J, Therrien M (2020).** "ERK signalling: a master regulator of cell behaviour, life and fate." *Nat Rev Mol Cell Biol* 21:607-632.
- **Kolch W (2005).** "Coordinating ERK/MAPK signalling through scaffolds and inhibitors." *Nat Rev Mol Cell Biol* 6:827-837.

### PI3K-Akt

- **Cantley LC (2002).** "The phosphoinositide 3-kinase pathway." *Science* 296:1655-1657.
- **Manning BD, Toker A (2017).** "AKT/PKB Signaling: Navigating the Network." *Cell* 169:381-405.
- **Hoxhaj G, Manning BD (2020).** "The PI3K-AKT network at the interface of oncogenic signalling and cancer metabolism." *Nat Rev Cancer* 20:74-88.

### AMPK

- **Hardie DG (2014).** "AMPK--sensing energy while talking to other signaling pathways." *Cell Metab* 20:939-952.
- **Garcia D, Shaw RJ (2017).** "AMPK: Mechanisms of Cellular Energy Sensing and Restoration of Metabolic Balance." *Mol Cell* 66:789-800.

## Primary Literature — Kinase Inhibitors (Chapter 9)

- **Druker BJ et al. (2001).** "Efficacy and safety of a specific inhibitor of the BCR-ABL tyrosine kinase in chronic myeloid leukemia." *N Engl J Med* 344:1031-1037. Original imatinib clinical paper.
- **Liu Y, Gray NS (2006).** "Rational design of inhibitors that bind to inactive kinase conformations." *Nat Chem Biol* 2:358-364. Type I/II classification.
- **Roskoski R Jr (2016).** "Classification of small molecule protein kinase inhibitors based upon the structures of their drug-target complexes." *Pharmacol Res* 103:26-48. Type I-VI scheme.
- **Cohen P, Cross D, Janne PA (2021).** "Kinase drug discovery 20 years after imatinib: progress and future directions." *Nat Rev Drug Discov* 20:551-569.
- **Wu P, Nielsen TE, Clausen MH (2015).** "FDA-approved small-molecule kinase inhibitors." *Trends Pharmacol Sci* 36:422-439.
- **Soria JC et al. (2018).** "Osimertinib in untreated EGFR-mutated advanced non-small-cell lung cancer." *N Engl J Med* 378:113-125. Key osimertinib trial.
- **O'Hare T et al. (2009).** "AP24534, a pan-BCR-ABL inhibitor for chronic myeloid leukemia, potently inhibits the T315I mutant and overcomes mutation-based resistance." *Cancer Cell* 16:401-412. Ponatinib design.

## Structural Databases

- **WebQC 3D molecular viewer** — interactive small-molecule structure pages. Example used in Chapter 2: [Ribose 3D molecular structure](https://www.webqc.org/3d-molecular-structure-of-Ribose.html).
- **PDB (Protein Data Bank, https://rcsb.org)** — every kinase structure cited above is downloadable here. Useful entries:
  - 1ATP — first PKA-ATP-substrate complex
  - 1IEP — ABL-imatinib complex (DFG-out)
  - 1M17 — EGFR-erlotinib
  - 4HVD — BRAF-vemurafenib
  - 4ANG, 6CER — ATP synthase whole complex
- **Kinase.com** — Manning lab kinome resources, phylogenetic trees, sequence alignments.
- **PhosphoSitePlus (https://phosphosite.org)** — curated phosphorylation site database.
- **KLIFS (Kinase-Ligand Interaction Fingerprints and Structures)** — kinase structure database with conformation annotations.
